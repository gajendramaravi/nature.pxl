Lao Tzu · The Tao Te Ching
01
Light is the True Subject
Every nature photograph is really a portrait of light. The creature, the leaf, the horizon — they are only vessels. Chase the light, and the world follows.
02
Stillness is the Deepest Skill
The decisive moment in nature is not pressed — it is earned through absolute stillness. The wild rewards those who become part of it, not those who intrude.
03
Witness Without Harm
Enter gently, observe carefully, leave nothing changed. The best image leaves the subject untouched and the viewer transformed.

Behind the Lens
Nature Photographer · Korba, Chhattisgarh, India

I'm a self-taught nature photographer rooted in Korba, Chhattisgarh — a land of coal and forests, ancient rivers and skies that burn long after the sun dips below the horizon. Photography found me quietly, somewhere between the dense canopy of Bastar and the sacred ruins of Sirpur, where nature doesn't perform — it simply exists.

My lens has wandered through the tribal heartlands of Dantewada, the painted skies of Rajasthan, and the coastal warmth of Maharashtra. Each place left something behind — a colour, a stillness, a truth. I don't chase photographs. I wait for the world to reveal itself, one frame at a time.
